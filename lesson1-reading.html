Lesson 1: Origins of the Cold War

Today I will

identify the 3 causes of the Cold War

So that I can

explain why the United States and Soviet Union became rivals

I know I’ve got it when I can

describe all 3 causes in my own words

Key Vocabulary

Cold War – a long conflict without direct war between the U.S. and USSR

Capitalism – an economic system based on private ownership

Communism – a system where the government controls the economy

Containment – the U.S. policy of stopping communism from spreading

Superpower – a very powerful country with global influence

Reading

After World War II, two powerful countries remained: the United States and the Soviet Union. During the war, they had been allies, but they never fully trusted each other.

The United States was suspicious of Joseph Stalin and the communist government of the Soviet Union. The Soviet Union was angry that the United States did not help them quickly enough during World War II. Because of this, both sides already had distrust before the Cold War fully began.

Think: Why would past problems during World War II make it harder for the two countries to work together after the war?

The first major cause of the Cold War was different ideology. The United States believed in democracy and capitalism. The Soviet Union believed in communism. Each side believed its own system was better and saw the other as a threat.

The second cause was distrust. The war had ended, but fear had not. The United States worried that the Soviet Union wanted to spread communism. The Soviet Union worried that the United States wanted to weaken or surround them.

Think: Why would two countries with such different beliefs find it hard to trust each other?

The third cause was competition for power. After World War II, Europe was weak and damaged. Both countries wanted influence in Europe and around the world. The Soviet Union pushed communist influence into Eastern Europe, while the United States wanted to stop that spread.

This led the United States to support containment, a policy meant to stop communism from growing into more countries. The Soviet Union, on the other hand, wanted to protect and expand its own influence.

Think: Why would both countries care so much about what happened in Europe after the war?

In simple terms, the Cold War began because of 3 big causes:

  • different ideologies
  • distrust after World War II
  • competition for power
Final Think: Which of the 3 causes do you think mattered most?

Main Idea

The Cold War started because the United States and Soviet Union had different beliefs, did not trust each other, and both wanted more power after World War II.
