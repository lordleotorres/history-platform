High School Social Studies

U.S. History Since 1877

This course follows the Texas U.S. History pathway from Reconstruction’s aftermath to the present, combining major eras, recurring TEKS skills, and STAR-focused coverage into a full-year structure.

Course Focus

Students trace how the United States changed politically, economically, socially, and culturally from 1877 to the present while practicing source analysis, historical writing, maps, charts, claims, and civic discourse.

  • Cover every major era from 1877 to the present
  • Keep the sequence aligned to TEKS and STAR expectations
  • Build recurring practice with primary and secondary sources
  • Embed economics, government, geography, and citizenship throughout
  • Use visual review, timelines, and turning points to strengthen retention
Course pathway

Start Here

Begin with foundational U.S. History review and the turning points after 1877, then move unit by unit through industrialization, reform, world wars, the Cold War, civil rights, and modern America.
