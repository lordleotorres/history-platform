Reading

After World War II, two powerful countries remained: the United States and the Soviet Union.

The United States believed in capitalism and democracy. The Soviet Union believed in communism.

These two systems were very different, and neither side trusted the other.

After the war, both countries wanted influence over Europe. This created tension.

The Cold War began because of three main causes:

  • Different ideologies
  • Competition for power
  • Fear and mistrust
