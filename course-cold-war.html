Reading

After World War II, two powerful countries remained: the United States and the Soviet Union.

During the war, they were allies. However, they never fully trusted each other. The Soviet Union believed the United States waited too long to help during the war. The United States did not trust the Soviet leader, Joseph Stalin.

Think: Why would this create tension between the two countries?

The two countries believed in very different systems.

The United States supported capitalism and democracy. People could vote, own businesses, and have freedoms.

The Soviet Union supported communism. The government controlled the economy and had more power over people.

Think: Why might each country believe their system is better?

After the war, both countries wanted power.

Europe was weak and damaged. Both the United States and Soviet Union wanted influence over these countries.

The Soviet Union began spreading communism in Eastern Europe. The United States wanted to stop communism from spreading.

Think: Why would both countries want control over Europe?

Because of these issues, the Cold War began.

  • Different ideologies
  • Distrust from World War II
  • Competition for power
Final Think: Which cause do you think mattered most?
