Map Challenge: NATO, Warsaw Pact, and Neutral States

Sort the countries, color the map, then reflect on how alliances divided Europe during the Cold War.

Instructions

How this works

  • First, drag each country into NATO, Warsaw Pact, or Neutral / Other.
  • Then choose a color mode and click the countries on the Europe map.
  • Blue means NATO, red means Warsaw Pact, and gray means Neutral / Other.
  • Important: the United States, Canada, East Germany, and West Germany appear in the sorting challenge, but not on the Europe map portion.
  • When you are done, press Check Answers. Your reflection questions will appear after that.
Step 1

Sort the countries

NATO

United States led alliance with Western Europe and Canada.

Warsaw Pact

Soviet led alliance of communist states in Eastern Europe.

Neutral / Other

Countries that did not belong to either Cold War alliance bloc.

Step 2

Color the map

Choose a color, then click countries on the map. This is a Europe-only map, so the United States and Canada are not shown here.

Reflection

Check for understanding

1. What is the difference between NATO and the Warsaw Pact?

2. Why did these alliances make the Cold War more dangerous?

3. Why might countries still choose to join alliances instead of standing alone?
